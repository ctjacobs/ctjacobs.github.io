Home

Hello. I'm Christian Jacobs, a postdoctoral researcher in the Applied Modelling and Computation Group at Imperial College London.

My research interests primarily reside in the field of computational fluid dynamics, where I develop advanced numerical modelling techniques to better understand fascinating geophysical flow phenomena. I am a core developer of Fluidity (a computational fluid dynamics framework featuring adaptive unstructured mesh capabilities) which I use to simulate explosive volcanic eruptions and understand the subsequent formation of ash plumes.

More recently I have developed an interest in research data management, particularly the issues surrounding reproducibility and data provenance in scientific computing. I am the lead developer of the PyRDM project which assists researchers in publishing their software source code and data to online, persistent and citable repositories.

For more information on my research, please visit the research page and list of software projects.

In addition to research, I also have a passion for teaching. I regularly deliver computing and mathematics-based courses at undergraduate and postgraduate level. I am also a certified Software Carpentry Instructor, helping to teach basic computational skills to scientists, and an Associate Fellow of the Higher Education Academy.

On the recreational side of things, I have a strong interest in amateur radio (also known as ham radio). I have been licensed since 2012 and currently hold the callsign 2E0ICL.
